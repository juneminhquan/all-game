Foods and Dining with Family

Primary School Level

Vocabulary Words

Breakfast

The first meal of the day, usually eaten in the morning.

Lunch

A meal eaten in the middle of the day.

Dinner

The main meal of the day, often eaten in the evening with family.

Healthy

Food that is good for your body and helps you grow strong.

Utensils

Tools like forks, spoons, and knives that we use to pick up and eat food.

Napkin

A piece of cloth or paper used to keep your face and hands clean while eating.

Recipe

A set of instructions that tells you how to cook a specific dish.

Dessert

A sweet treat, like cake or fruit, eaten at the end of a meal.

Manners

Being polite at the table, such as saying "please" and "thank you."

Appetite

The feeling of being hungry and ready to eat.

Balanced

A meal that has many different types of good food, like vegetables, fruit, and protein.

Crunchy

Food that makes a snapping sound when you bite it, like a carrot or a cracker.

Feast

A very large and special meal with many different kinds of food.

Leftovers

Food that is not finished during a meal and is saved to be eaten later.

Set the table

To get the table ready for a meal by putting out plates, cups, and forks.

Ingredients

The different items, like flour, eggs, or salt, that are mixed together to make food.

Delicious

When something tastes very, very good.

Chew

To use your teeth to break up food before you swallow it.

Sharing

Letting others have some of your food or passing dishes around the table.

Grains

Foods like bread, rice, and pasta that give you energy.

Read and Answer

A Day of Delicious Meals

"Leo started his day with a big breakfast. He ate grains like cereal to give him energy for school. For lunch, Leo ate a healthy sandwich and a crunchy carrot. It was a balanced meal that made him feel strong. In the evening, the family prepared for dinner. Leo helped set the table by putting out the utensils and a napkin for everyone. Mom followed a special recipe. She mixed many ingredients to make a delicious chicken stew. Because the family was all together, it felt like a feast. Leo had a great appetite and began to chew his food slowly. He showed his best manners by sharing the side dishes and saying 'thank you.' After the main meal, they all enjoyed a sweet dessert of fruit and yogurt. They put the leftovers in the fridge to eat the next day."

1. What did Leo eat for breakfast to give him energy?

Answer: Leo ate grains, like cereal.

2. What two things did Leo put out when he helped set the table?

Answer: He put out utensils and a napkin for everyone.

3. How did Leo show he had good manners during dinner?

Answer: He showed good manners by sharing the side dishes and saying 'thank you.'

4. What did the family do with the food they did not finish?

Answer: They put the leftovers in the fridge to eat the next day.

5. Why did the dinner feel like a feast?

Answer: It felt like a feast because the family was all together and there were many kinds of food.

Listen and Answer

"Every day starts with a healthy breakfast, usually eggs and grains like toast to give us energy. After a quick lunch at school, I come home with a big appetite for dinner. Today, Mom follows a new recipe, and I help her gather all the ingredients. My job is to set the table with plates, utensils, and a clean napkin for everyone. We sit together for a balanced meal that looks like a giant feast. I remember to use my manners by sharing the side dishes and saying 'please.' The crunchy carrots make a loud sound as I chew them, and everything tastes delicious. After we finish, we put the leftovers in the fridge and enjoy a sweet dessert of fresh fruit."

1. What does the speaker usually eat for breakfast to get energy?

The script states that every day starts with eggs and grains like toast to provide energy.

2. What is the speaker's specific job when preparing for dinner?

The speaker explicitly mentions, 'My job is to set the table with plates, utensils, and a clean napkin for everyone.'

3. How does the speaker demonstrate good manners during the meal?

The speaker says they remember their manners by sharing the side dishes and saying 'please.'

4. Which food item makes a loud sound when the speaker chews it?

The text mentions that 'the crunchy carrots make a loud sound as I chew them.'

5. What do the family members eat for dessert after the meal?

At the end of the script, it says they 'enjoy a sweet dessert of fresh fruit.'
